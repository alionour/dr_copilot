Privacy Policy

Last Updated: December 09, 2025

1. Introduction

Welcome to Dr. Copilot ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal and clinical data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our desktop application and related services (the "Service").

2. Data We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

We may collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you register for the Service, specifically:

  • Name and Contact Information (Email address)
  • Professional Credentials (Clinic name, specialty)
  • Payment Information (Processed securely via our payment provider, Paddle)

2.2 Patient Data (Protected Health Information)

Dr. Copilot processes patient data entered by you. This data is:

  • Encrypted locally on your device or in our secure cloud (if Backup is enabled).
  • Processed by AI providers (like OpenAI or Google) only via HIPAA-compliant and/or enterprise agreements where data is not used for model training.

2.3 Usage Data

We verify subscription status and application version, but we do not track individual patient interactions for marketing purposes.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide and maintain the Service (e.g., generating clinical reports, transcribing audio).
  • Process payments and manage your subscription.
  • Send administrative information, such as updates and security alerts.
  • Enforce our Terms of Service.

4. Data Storage and Security

We implement industry-standard security measures, including encryption at rest and in transit, to protect your data. Your clinical data is stored in secure cloud infrastructure (AWS/Google Cloud) with strict access controls.

5. Sharing of Information

We do not sell your personal data. We disclose information only:

  • To Service Providers: We use trusted third-party vendors (e.g., Paddle for payments, Firebase for authentication) who assist us in operating the Service.
  • For Legal Reasons: We may disclose information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities.

6. Your Rights

You have the right to access, correct, or delete your personal information stored in our systems. You can manage your data directly within the application settings.
